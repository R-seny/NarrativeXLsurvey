Consent to Participate in Research


Version date: 05/18/2023


Purpose of the research:

You are being asked to participate in a research project that is being done by Arseny Moskvichev, from the Sante Fe Institute and Ky-Vinh Mai, from the University of California, Irvine. We would you to complete a survey to learn more about narrative understanding, which will be extended to neural machine translation for language models. The interview will last about 20 - 30 minutes. You are eligible to participate because you live in the US and are at least 18 years old.

This consent form contains important information about this project and what to expect if you decide to participate. Please consider the information carefully. Feel free to ask questions before making your decision whether or not to participate. Your participation in this research is voluntary.

What you will do in the project:

You will participate in this research using your own computer, in the place of your own choosing. The research will involve reading book snippets and filling in simple multiple choice questions about corresponding book summaries on whether or not they are accurate to the snippet. You will be able to end your participation at any moment, although you will only receive compensation if you complete the experiment.

Risks:

There are no known risks associated with this study, other than a mild fatigue associated with reading multiple paragraphs of text and potential mild frustration related to answering some questions.

Benefits:

Other than the monetary compensation, there are no known benefits in participating in this research. However, it is hoped that some of the narrative texts and summaries may be interesting and intellectually stimulating.

Confidentiality of your information:

We will not store any personally identifiable information, except for your MTurk ID, which we need to process payments. This information will first be stored on a password protected server, then (when the data collection process is over) will be transferred onto a password-protected laboratory computer. We will remove your MTurk ID from the data before releasing it to the research community. We will take measures to protect the security of all your personal information, but we cannot guarantee confidentiality of all research data. The University of New Mexico Institutional Review Board (IRB) that oversees human research and/or other entities (such as a Sponsor or FDA) may be permitted to access your records. Your name will not be used in any published reports about this project.

You should understand that the researchers are not prevented from taking steps, including reporting to authorities, to prevent serious harm to yourself or others.

Use of your information for future research: All identifiable information (which only includes your MTurk ID) will be removed from the information or samples collected in this project. After we remove all identifiers, the information or samples may be used for future research or shared with other researchers without your additional informed consent.

Payment:

Completing the task takes, on average, around 20-30 minutes although it might take a longer or shorter period of time depending on your individual pace. In return for your time participating in this project, you will be paid $5.00. You will not be paid and your task will be rejected if you do not complete the project, or your activity are suspicious or your answers are nonsensical.

Right to withdraw from the research:

Your participation in this research is completely voluntary. You have the right to choose not to participate or to withdraw your participation at any time without penalty. In the course of the experiment, there may be up to three simple “attention check” problems that we will use to check if you are paying attention during the experiment. If you fail them, the experiment will be terminated without payment. After you begin the experiment, you will be able to end your participation at any moment, although you will only receive compensation if you complete the experiment. If you have any questions, concerns, or complaints about the research, please contact mm@santafe.edu . If you have questions regarding your rights as a research participant, or about what you should do in case of any research-related harm to you, or if you want to obtain information or offer input, please contact the IRB. The IRB is a group of people from the University of New Mexico and the community who provide independent oversight of safety and ethical issues related to research involving people: UNM Office of the IRB, (505) 277-2644, irbmaincampus@unm.edu. Website: http://irb.unm.edu/
